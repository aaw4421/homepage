advice for reffing streamed matches

back to all guides

If you're reffing a streamed match, you're probably doing fine, but if you want to go above and beyond, take note of these tips (which, really, are all just my opinion based on experience)!

  • As soon as you make the lobby, !addref the streamer. It also helps to ping the streamer on Discord telling them they're addreffed.

  • An optional courtesy is to create an "empty lobby" with the tourney abbreviation, like: !mp make XYZ: (empty) vs (lobby). Switching to that lobby and back can fix issues in osu!tourney.

  • Make sure to promptly post the mp link or ID to the ref sheet and optionally send it to the streamer in Discord too. It gets added to a Twitch command and some streamers also join the lobby on IRC.

  • Unless told otherwise by hosts/admins, do not delay a match just because stream isn't ready yet. Stream can catch up later, and many players don't appreciate delays.

  • You have complete control of the song that plays on stream, using !mp map [id]. You may use this for good or evil. Some refs like to put on the TB while teams are rolling and banning.

  • Even during warmups, there must be a player in slot 1 of the lobby for the tourney client.

  • Streamers may not know who "osuplayer423" is when they say "ban nm2". When asking for bans, use the team name or color rather than the captain's name, so the streamer can assign bans correctly!

  • Even if a team "instapicks" after a map, give some time before actually changing the map for the benefit of the commentators and streamer.

  • Watch the stream, if you can! Sometimes a player has tech issues at the start of a map, but maybe doesn't know they can ask for an !mp abort.

  • This is an obvious one, but just keep the ref sheet updated. If a streamer missed something or made a mistake in the overlay, they may use the ref sheet to check picks, bans, and winners.

  • Pay attention for Discord pings from commentators and streamers, which may alert you to issues or questions.

  • Especially when a team "instapicks", don't forget to post the current score and pick, which can act as a "sanity check" for the streamer.
